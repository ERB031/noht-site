There are stories that entertain. There are stories that inspire. And then there are stories that the world simply cannot afford not to know.

NOHT Film Productions is honored to announce our partnership with The Elizabeth Foundation Atlanta — a nonprofit organization at the forefront of homeless advocacy in our city — to document their remarkable collaboration with the Emory University Nursing School. Together, these two organizations are doing something urgent and profound: meeting unhoused people in Atlanta where they are, delivering direct medical care, and fighting for the dignity and survival of some of our community's most vulnerable neighbors.

We are here to make sure that work is seen.

**The Mission**

Atlanta's unhoused population faces a healthcare crisis that most of us never witness. Without stable shelter, access to consistent medical care becomes nearly impossible — which means conditions that are entirely treatable become life-threatening, and lives that could be saved are lost.

The Elizabeth Foundation is working to change that. Through their partnership with Emory's nursing program, they are bringing trained medical professionals directly to the people who need them — on the street, in the margins, in the places that formal healthcare systems rarely reach. It is compassionate, courageous, and clinically rigorous work carried out by people who have made the deliberate choice to show up for those the city too often overlooks.

That commitment deserves a story equal to its weight.

**What NOHT Will Do**

Our role in this partnership is to document — honestly, humanely, and with the full force of our craft — what this work actually looks like on the ground.

Not the polished version. Not the version designed to be comfortable from a safe distance. The real version: the nurses kneeling on pavement, the volunteers building trust with people who have every reason not to give it, the moments of connection and care that happen in the spaces between institutions and infrastructure.

We will follow this work with patience and deep respect for everyone in the frame — most importantly the unhoused individuals at the center of it, whose humanity and dignity will guide every creative decision we make. Their stories are not ours to own. They are ours to honor.

The films, portraits, and documentary content we create together will be used to raise awareness, build support, and expand the reach of The Elizabeth Foundation's mission — bringing more eyes, more resources, and more community investment to work that is already saving lives.

**On the Responsibility of the Camera**

Documenting homelessness requires a particular kind of care. It is easy to make poverty cinematic in ways that are, at bottom, exploitative — that aestheticize suffering, that reduce human beings to symbols of a social problem, that leave the audience feeling moved without being moved to act.

That is not what we are here to make.

NOHT's approach to this work is grounded in relationship first, camera second. We will spend time with The Elizabeth Foundation and their Emory partners before we shoot anything. We will listen far more than we speak. We will ask hard questions about representation, consent, and impact at every stage. And we will hold ourselves accountable to a simple standard: does this story serve the people in it?

If the answer is yes, we roll. If it isn't yet, we keep working until it is.

**Why Atlanta. Why Now.**

This city is ours. Its struggles belong to all of us, and so does its capacity to respond to them with creativity, compassion, and real resources.

NOHT was built on the belief that visual storytelling is one of the most powerful forces we have for shifting how people see — and therefore how they act. Partnering with The Elizabeth Foundation is us putting that belief to work in the most direct way we know how. Not abstractly. Not theoretically. Here, in Atlanta, right now, for people whose lives are on the line.

We are showing up. We are paying attention. And we are going to tell this story in a way that demands the same from everyone who watches it.

**To The Elizabeth Foundation and Emory Nursing — Thank You**

Thank you for the work you do every single day, long before any camera arrives. Thank you for letting us in. Thank you for trusting us with something this important.

We will not waste it.
